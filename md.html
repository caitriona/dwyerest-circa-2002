Martin Dwyer

Born in Cork in 1949 he is the youngest of seven children, he was educated in Cork and left U.C.D. with a B.A. in English and History in 1971.

He first worked as a Chef in Snaffles Restaurant in Dublin, then in the Chateau de Teildras in Anjou in France.

He spent some years working in the "Wife of Bath" restaurant in Kent before coming back to Ireland in 1976.

He then worked in several restaurants in the South East; the Galley on the river in New Ross, Ballinakill House in Waterford and the Strand Inn in Dunmore East.

Martin opened his own restaurant "Dwyers" on Mary St. in Waterford in 1989 and has continued as Chef and Proprietor of this critically acclaimed restaurant since.

Martin has been co-running the Ballyhack Cookery Centre, which he founded with Pierce and Valerie McAuliffe of the Neptune Restaurant, across the river from Waterford, in Ballyhack, Co. Wexford, for the last three years.

He is also the resident Chef on Waterford Local Radio with a weekly slot on Tuesday mornings at 11.30am.

Martin is also a commissioner for Eurotoques - the European body of Chefs dedicated to the highest standards of excellence in food and professional cooking - and has represented that society at meetings in Belgium and Sweden.

Recently he has taken on the job as food consultant to a local family run supermarket in Waterford, Ardkeen Stores.

In his spare time he confesses to an addiction to crosswords.

Martin has appeared in RTE's cookery program Pot Luck.
